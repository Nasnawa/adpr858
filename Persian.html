work samples/Persian

TV Recorded Reports

Kabul's crime at night

In the first three months of this year in Kabul, the capital of Afghanistan more than 600 crimes were recorded. Kabul police says, 580 people have been arrested for involvement in the crimes and most of these crimes occur at night.

First Afghanistan's biometric election

I went to a polling centre in Kabul and explained the whole process of voting which was done by biometric device for the first time in Afghanistan. I went close to a polling centre and talked with people about the problems and their complaints who risked their life to vote..

War affects in Afghanistan; PTSD

The number of army and police forces on the battlefield reaches to more than 300000 who don’t have access to mental health services. Most of those who face violent scenes of war are at risk for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). I interviewed some prior police officers and war doctors who are now suffering PTSD and pointed out the risky situation for all afghan people who are experiencing some signs of this disorder like journalists.

Consequences of Afghanistan's War for Children: 'Over 70% of children suffer anxiety and depression'

A recent survey which was released by the Save the Children organization shows that more than 70 percent of children in Afghanistan are anxious in result of war and insecurity. The survey interviewed 600 parents and 90 children in four provinces of Afghanistan. Many of interviewers said their children feel insecure when going to school and shop and when approaching a government building or checkpoint or even playing with their friends outside feel insecure.

Taliban attack on ministry of technology

How Taliban attacked the ministry of information and technology and what happened inside the clashes for hours? I went to the area and illustrated the incident.

Afghans want peace

A recent survey by the Asian Foundation in Afghanistan shows that nearly 90 percent of the population in the country supports peace talks between the government and the Taliban. Also, more than half of those interviewed said they are hopeful about the outcome of the peace talks. The survey was based on interviews with nearly 18000 people over the age of 18, and about 50 percent of participants were women.

TV live Reports

Attack on Helmand

About 40 civillians were killed in hawayi and zamini attacks in Musa Qala district of Helmand, last night. The governor says the target was Alqaeda and Taliban members and 14 were killed.

Violence and casualities scalation of civillians

Afghan Human Rights Commission more than 11000 civilians were victim of war and violence through the last year. The statistics show 19 % increase of causalities. 3000 died and more than 8000 were injured.

The taliban banned schools for girls in their controlled area of Sarepol

Afghanistan’s government says that Taliban have hindered girls to go to school in 6 districts of Sare Pol province where is under their control. They don’t allow girls to study more than elementary level. Local sources confirm that Taliban threated teachers to not allow girls in schools otherwise they will be killed.

Afghanistan's futsal team got second in Asian champions

Afghanistan’s Under-20 Futsal team got the second position in Asian champions. Japan won 3-1 in their final match in Tabriz. Afghanistan’s Futsal had reached the final for the first time, and this made thousands of people welcome them inside and outside the country.
